Children

The Future Generation

The disparity in tech readiness for Latino students begins with underfunded K-12 schools, which limit access to essential STEM resources and early exposure to technology. This digital inequality extends beyond devices to inadequate internet and training, leaving many students unprepared for tech careers. To address this, policymakers must prioritize equitable funding for Latino-majority schools, improve digital infrastructure, and invest in accessible STEM-focused programs, including affordable summer initiatives, to foster engagement and inspire a generation of tech innovators.
